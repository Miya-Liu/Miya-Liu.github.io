Purchase Allocation Problem Analysis

The formalization of the purchase allocation problem in paper
Best Bang for the Buck: Purchase Allocation Under AccessDependent Pricing Functions.

01/06/2021 Mengya Liu Updated 1 day ago

Problem Formalization:

To formalize the purchase allocation problem in the presence of ADPF, first, we define the subset of the full answer as Allocation. To measure the utility of an allocation, inspired by the work of FedMark, we define the concept of the value of a solution, \(v(s)\), to capture the willingness of clients to pay for \(s\) based on their preferences. Then, the utility of an allocation \(\Lambda\) is $$\mu(\Lambda)=\sum_{s\in\Lambda}v(s).$$ Let \(r(s) \in \{0,1\}\) denote whether a solution \(s\) is in \(\Lambda\) or not. If decide to allocate \(s\), it has to purchase all tuples in \(RT(s)\). Let \(z(t)\in\{0,1\}\) represent whether a tuple \(t\) is purchased or not. Thus, the above constraint is \(r_{s}=1\iff \forall t\in RT(s), z(t)=1\), which can be formalized by $$|RT(s)|\times r(s) - \sum_{t\in RT(s)}z(t) \leq 0.$$ Since the same triple can exist in different sources with different prices, whichever data sources clients choose to purchase from, they should pay only once for it, even if it contributes to multiple solutions. Therefore, PA has to track where each triple has been purchased. To do so, we introduce the navigation matrix, a (0,1)-matrix \(DT=(a_{i,j})_{m\times n}\), where \(m=|D_{M}|\) and \(n=|RTA(\Lambda)|\), to represent the purchase of the triples in \(RTA(\Lambda)\) over \(D^{M}\). The binary variable \(a_{ij} \in \left\{0,1\right\}\) denotes whether PA purchases the \(j\)th triple \(t_{j}\) in \(RTA(\Lambda)\) from \(d_{i}\). Thus, the pay-only-once constraint is $$z(t_{j}) = \sum_{i=1}^{m}a_{ij} \leq 1,$$ where \(j = 1, \cdots, n.\) The cost of the triples purchased from \(d_{i}\) is $$pf\Big(\{t_{j}|a_{ij}=1, j = 1, \dots, n\}, d_{i}\Big).$$ Then, the cost of \(\Lambda\) under the budget constraint is $$\sum_{i=1}^{m}pf\big(\{t_{j}|a_{ij}=1, j = 1, \dots, n\},d_{i}\big) \leq B.$$ Hence, purchase allocation is formalized into: $$\begin{equation} \label{eq:target} \begin{gathered} \Upsilon: \operatorname*{arg\,max}_{\Lambda \subseteq S} \mu(\Lambda) \\ \text{s.t. } z(t_{j}) = \sum_{i=1}^{m}a_{ij} \leq 1, t_{j} \in RTA(\Lambda), j = 1, \cdots, n, \\ |RT(s)|\times r(s) - \sum_{t\in RT(s)}z(t) \leq 0, \forall s \in \Lambda \\ \sum_{i=1}^{m}pf\big(\{t_{j}|a_{ij}=1, j = 1, \dots, n\}, d_{i}\big) \leq B \\ a_{ij} \in \left\{0,1\right\}, i = 1, ...,m, j = 1, ...,n\\ \end{gathered} \end{equation}$$
