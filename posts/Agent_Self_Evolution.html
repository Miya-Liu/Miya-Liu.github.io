Can you tell that your agent is evolving?

— after an evaluation on agent's self-evolution performance.

20/04/2026 Miya Liu

In the field of AI, especially the recent area of AI agents, the future and endevor is to see that an agent can grow up like a human being, i.e. having an agent onboarding at basic level and growing into an expert in a manner of self-evolution. However, apart from telling the growth of your agent by your gut feeling, there is not much systematic way to actually evaluate the self-evolution of your agent. For instance, did agent learn from past experiences, have it enhanced its skills without affecting previous capabilities, is it getting formaliser with its owners or users, etc.

In this page, I will share my ongoing study log about evaluating agents capability growth from its self-evolution. The evaluation shall first propose a group of dimensions to measure agent evolution, followed by different policies to build datasets and conduct experiments to report performance analysis with full rationale and evidence in each dimension.

What Self-Evolution Means

  • Not one-shot fine-tuning, but iterative adaptation in running systems.
  • Signals come from failures, corrections, confirmations, and repeated tasks.
  • Outputs include updated memory, adjusted prompts, and extracted skills.

Core Loop to Study

  1. Observe outcomes (successes, failures, friction points).
  2. Extract structured learning from transcripts and tool traces.
  3. Classify learning (memory update, rule update, skill candidate).
  4. Validate changes with tests or replay tasks.
  5. Promote reliable learnings and monitor drift.

Memory and Skill Evolution

  • Short-term memory supports immediate context adaptation.
  • Project/team memory captures durable conventions and known pitfalls.
  • Reusable skills are distilled from repeated, verified learnings.

Evaluation Dimensions

  • Task success and recovery quality after errors.
  • Human supervision load per completed task.
  • Stability over time (avoiding regressions and behavior drift).
  • Cost-performance balance under realistic workloads.

Guardrails

  • Do not auto-promote uncertain learnings into global rules.
  • Keep traceability from behavior change to source evidence.
  • Use staged rollout for major policy or prompt updates.
